About Me

Hi everybody! My name is Billy Henshaw, and I am a recent graduate of the MSGIS program at Clark University. I graduated from Allegheny College in 2018 with a B.S. in Environmental Science with a love for GIS. I chose to pursue an Atmospheric Science Ph.D. with the goal of combining GIS and climate science, but ultimately this goal fell through as my research did not prove to be socially impactful. Thus, I enrolled in Clark University for its emphasis on grooming students into world leaders in sustainable development and GIS. I have a background in Environmental Science and a goal to increase public awareness of climate change impacts through data visualization and modeling.

My love for geospatial and sustainable development stems from the desire to merge technology with humanitarian aid. I recognize the potential to deliver powerful results using geospatial data, in all phases of project management, including monitoring and evaluation. For example, spatial analysis can assist with project closeout (how much have the beneficiaries/land areas changed throughout the project), project proposals (what is affecting the study region and how can this problem be resolved), and data collection (survey data aggregation and display). I love GIS because it is a tool that can solve both quantitative and qualitative problems.

I define myself as a motivated problem-solver who loves giving back to the community and preserving the natural world. In GIS and project management, efficiency saves time and money, which are everything. Thus, I currently strive to find ways to make workflows more efficient and innovative.

My professional interests include sustainable development, climate change impacts, smart agriculture, science communication, machine learning, and raster analysis. I am particularly interested in the use of machine learning and automated geoprocessing to create datasets at a finer spatial and temporal resolution than those coming from satellite imagery.

The dream job involves collecting field data in places I have never been, collaborating with brilliant minds, creating impactful products, and growing and learning every day. This job would be at a company that not only exists at the cutting edge of geospatial technology but also provides services that create positive social change.

When I am not working, you can find me playing Ultimate Frisbee, chess, Dungeons & Dragons, or hiking in the Pacific Northwest.

My Interests

Climate Change

We are on track for a 2.4 °C increase in warming above pre-industrial levels (Link). How can we visualize the threats climate change poses?

Smart Agriculture

As global temperatures and populations increase, croplands are under immense stress. How can we use GIS to help farmers maximize yields and improve the quality of their lands?

Sustainable Development

Sustainable development is the improvement of overall quality of life for everyone as a means of saving the planet from climate disaster. Every solution is needed to combat this dangerous threat to our society. GIS plays a pivotal role in this.

Science Communication

Science cannot live in a vacuum. The public must learn from the scientists to enact change. GIS and spatial data visualization are often an efficient way to communicate powerful results. After all, a picture is worth a thousand words.
